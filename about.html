10 reasons why you need a music website


Have you been debating whether or not you need a website for your music? With social media freely and easily accessible, you may have a great online presence already. So do you really need a website to stand out as a musician?


Yes! If you were to ask a music fan, or an industry expert how best to access your music and find out more about you as an artist, they would point to a dedicated website.


Here are 10 reasons why you need a music website.


1. Industry and media expect to find your website


Making a music website takes some time, effort, and a bit of know-how. If you’ve invested the time into creating a dedicated space online that includes professional photos, your music, bio, and looks great on mobile, then industry and the media will get the sense that you’re a serious musician. As a result they will take you, and your music, more seriously.


2. Everything is in one place


In addition, everyone from music bloggers to booking agents and venues, will often look to one place to find everything they need to know about you. You can host all of your music on your website, and point attention to your recent releases.


Plus, a press kit page on your website makes it easy for industry to get the information they need. If your bio, hi-res photos, and videos are easily accessible and laid out professionally, it increases your chances of getting booked.



3. A website lets you showcase your band's unique personality


Without being confined to a newsfeed or number of characters, a website can be anything you want it to be. You can base your design on your latest album, or just go with a vibe that your music evokes.


You can add as many or as few pages as you’d like. Your personality can leap off the screen through the images and text you choose. A music website will help someone make an impression of you - with a unique design that suits your music, you can create that impression.


4. Social media platforms come and go


Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram are great platforms to promote your band and your music. However, a website is permanent. You own the content, and control the experience completely. You aren't at the mercy of paid posts, and you can direct anybody, at any time, to visit your website. Not everyone is on Facebook - those people, if they are online, will still be able to visit your website.


5. You can sell music and merch directly to fans


If your fans want to support you, selling your music online to them directly is a great way. With a website, you can sell anything you’d like, and more of that money goes directly into your pocket to produce the next album (music sales on Bandzoogle are commission-free). You can swap out music or merch items whenever you like, set the pricing, and offer discounts.



6. It can help you book more gigs


Any booking agent or promoter will check out your website before booking you for a show. You can use this to your advantage by showcasing only your best work, including your most recent music and videos. You can also list out your previous shows.


With a professional look to your site, a booker is much more likely to get the sense that you’re a serious, qualified musician, and is in turn more likely to book you for a show.


7. It's better for SEO organic search results


With your own music website, you can add text to your pages that mention your artist name and your latest music. You can choose a domain name that comes from your artist or band name. As a result Google will match your band keywords to your website, giving it more authority than a social media account.


8. You own your data


With whatever platform you choose to build a website, you can collect email addresses and buyer information, and keep those details with you. Build up your fan base over time, and keep in touch with them when you release new music.


9. It’s one of the best ways to build your mailing list


Reaching out by email is still one of the best ways to create a direct connection with your fans. You can build up your mailing list by offering a free track in exchange for a new single. Keep in touch regularly by email to promote your upcoming events and releases. Segmenting by city will also help you maintain a strong presence when you reach out to fans ahead of a tour.


10. You have complete control


If you make your own music website, you can control everything about it. From the pages you add to the images you choose, you can update it at any time. Receive a nomination for a music award? Add that to your Homepage. Releasing a new album with a totally new vibe? Update your header image and section colors to match it.


All of the words on your music website come from you, so you can curate the content and arrange it exactly how you want to be seen online. Instead of a promoter grabbing a grainy or old Facebook profile picture, include hi-resolution photos in a variety of sizes, and they will make use of those instead.
